Until then ..... About Me

Gary Burgmann is a student software engineer and is currently working for Griffith Univeristy's eResearch team as a developer. His current focus at work is rebuilding a clinical trials application, from Plone to a SPA architecture of ReactJS/Pyramid/MySQL, where his role is to develop the API in Pyramid. It has been an interesting learning experience to apply continous integration with a Docker & Jenkins pipeline.

He is also developing a web application for the Queensland State Archives to assist in automatic reconstruction of document pieces, using Django as the framework and the JavaScript library Konva for the manipulation of individual pieces where needed. Previously he has also worked as a developer on a large project with an Angular/Laravel/MySQL stack.

Outside of work, he thoroughly enjoys working within the Django ecosystem and is currently developing two personal projects utilising the framework. He has previously deployed applications to Digital Ocean (including this one!), one of which was a ReactJS/ExpressJS/Mongo stack for his wedding to receive guest RSVPs and provide information for guests. Moving forward, his aim is to utilize serverless deployments with AWS, implementing the Serverless framework for NodeJS or Zappa for Django/Flask projects, a SPA architecture consisting of server-side rendered ReactJS with NextJS, and Amazon's Relational Database Service with Postgres, as his stack of choice. He is also very keen to learn more data science related techniques, having only briefly used SciKit learn modules for high level machine learning tasks in the past, now currently diving into PyTorch with Udacity.
